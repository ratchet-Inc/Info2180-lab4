How does it work?

Quick overview

The Reko Engine has a modular design which allows it to be implemented within a larger system as a subsystem. The engine is designed to collect data from users based on what content they consume. Recommendations are then made based on the data collected along with available data of movie titles. These recommendations are based on 4 concepts.

Concept 1:

People have preferences. An individual may like genre X & genre Y but greatly dislike genre Z. Utilizing this information the system then provides recommendations target X & Y but excluding Z.

Concept 2:

People have preferences, but they are not always equally preferred. Based on the data collected by the system, it can estimate how much more you like a criterion when compared to another. The result of applying this analogy provides more personalized recommendations.

Concept 3:

Though humans are unique in their own way, we tend to have common preferences. Recommendations are then made by finding users that are quite similar to each other and producing recommendations based on titles that are not commonly watched.

Concept 4:

Since humans generally have similar preferences, it can be safe to assume a user will like a title based on what another user consumes.
